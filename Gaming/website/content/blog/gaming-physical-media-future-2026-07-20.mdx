## The End of an Era: Physical Media in Gaming

This move has sparked a lively debate about the future of physical media in gaming, and the potential consequences for game preservation and legacy gaming.

One of the most vocal critics of this decision is Yoko Taro, the creator of the Nier series. In a recent post on X, Taro bemoaned the loss of physical media, citing the tactile experience of holding a game in your hands, and the sense of ownership that comes with it. He also noted that physical media provides a tangible connection to the past, and allows gamers to hold onto memories of their favorite childhood games.

## The Rise of Digital Distribution

While physical media may be on the decline, digital distribution has become the norm in the gaming industry. Platforms like Steam, GOG, and the Epic Games Store have made it easier than ever for gamers to purchase and download games directly to their computers. However, this shift towards digital distribution has also raised concerns about game preservation and legacy gaming.

With the rise of digital distribution, many classic games are being left behind, and are in danger of being lost forever. This is a problem that affects not just gamers, but also developers and publishers who want to preserve their legacy and ensure that their games continue to be enjoyed by future generations.
